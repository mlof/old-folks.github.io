Finnish Salmon Soup [by Sleep]

for lunch or dinner

Ingredients:

  • 6 potatoes
  • 1-2 carrots
  • some spring onions (about the same amount as 1-2 regular onions, you can also sub those)
  • 2 tbsp of butter
  • 2 tbsp of flour
  • 1 liter of fish broth (i used broth cubes, if you don't have that, you can just use 1 liter of water and throw in salt to taste, starting with 1-1,5 tsp and adding more once it's done if needed)
  • 8-10 whole allspice berries + like 5 whole peppercorns
  • 2dl of cream (any type will do really)
  • 400g of salmon, fresh or frozen
  • dill

Method:

  1. chop all veggies and fry them in the butter for about 2mins. add the flour and mix until it coats the veggies. add water + salt/fish broth and the pepper and allspice. boil until veggies are cooked.
  2. do anything you need to do to prepare the fish (skin+bones off) if using fresh fish and add the fish to the soup. boil for 3-5mins if using fresh fish and for about 10mins if frozen. also add the cream at this point. once that's done, taste and add dill to taste and also check if there's enough salt. finish with green spring onion ends. then enjoy, u did a food

Scottish 'Granny's Lentil Soup' [by Agent Scully]

for lunch or dinner

Ingredients:

  • 250g of red lentils
  • 4 carrots
  • Half a turnip
  • 2 chicken stock cubes
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Water

Method:

  1. Grate the carrot and turnip and add to a pot with the lentils and stock cubes on top
  2. Depending on how thick you like your soup, add water. For a thick soup, just add enough water to cover everything
  3. Bring to the boil for about 15 minutes then turn down to a medium heat to simmer until the lentils and veg are soft and cooked and the stock cubes have dissolved. Add salt and pepper to taste and more water if the soup is too thick
  4. Serve with some nice bread and enjoy!
